Porn Ban: What it actually brings

A welcome or a Wrath, lets see here..

Dilip Singh | August 4, 2015

This week saw uproar among the Indians with the news coming up “Porn banned in India”, thereby making it a trending topic across the Social Media. We saw people coming up protesting against by justifying it saying an interference with their privacy.

The government has asked the ISPs to block Porn sites and in the process about 857 sites are banned till date. Users of MTNL, BSNL have lost their access. However private broadband users are yet to receive the wrath.

I analysed the subject and came up with a conclusion which I guess will please both the groups, with and against the motion. But first let’s see the impact of Porn to Society and Human behaviour:

The For:

Freedom of Expression:

It’s about the freedom of expression and human physical need, doing so the government will deprive us of our Fundamental Right.

What consenting adults do is their business:

The sex industry needs to be regulated, not sent underground. Most of the problems stem from the belief that sex industry workers are not worthy of respect, and neither is their profession. But they do provide a service, and like it or not it's one which does fulfil human needs. They should not be denigrated for doing so.

Stop censoring the internet:

Why the hell should ANYONE tell us what we can and cannot see, can and cannot do on the internet. It's not just a matter of mature, pornographic content; it's a matter of censorship in general


The Cons:

Damaging the society and hurting our children's future:

Porn is similar to drugs and functions similarly like a drug. Pornography can damage relationships, destroy families, separate couples, etc. Also, the production of pornography is slowly beginning to strain our economy. Now, good quality movies do not strain economy, but pornography will one day cost our country immense amounts of money.

Diminishing Values and Increased sexual Assaults on Women:

Porn is an evil to the society. It is degrading gender relationships and objectify Women. This leads to sexual assaults on women and rape cases are rapidly increasing. Many porn Actors are brutally tortured and they were later forced to prostitution.

People respecting people:

Porn only highlights what they want you to believe that all women are like the women portrayed in these websites which are downgrading to real women who do not do any of the things which are portrayed by these other women.

Wrongly portrayed:

The type of sex found in some of these porn involving abnormal sexual activities, sex with people at a time, use of not only the objects but even animals for sex misleads people creating false expectation and illusion.

But the Idea of Ban won’t work!

The US and China also tried to fight porn and saw its results and then they only limited themselves to child pornography. Now comes even if the Indian Government is successful to ban, banning internet porn won’t work since:

  • Banning web content is not as easy as banning the IPs rather be said impossible.
  • You cannot ban the servers located in different countries.
  • Internet porn is just a smmall part of this dim light world.
  • It shall still be available on torrents which are the true download source.

Conclusion:

Hence we could conclude the ban will merely make the accessibility difficult but not impossible. Thus Children won’t be getting the links with just a simple Google Search like today. Adults with adequate internet knowledge and time shall be able to download and enjoy. Your hard-disk will be relived and love will be in the air not the porn ;) :P
